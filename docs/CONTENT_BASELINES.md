# Tweet Content Baselines - Founder Profiles

## Purpose
This document establishes baseline content profiles for each founder to identify when their tweet content is unusual or deviates from their normal patterns.

## Analysis Date
2025-12-20

---

## Founder Profiles

### 1. a1lon9 (PUMP) - "The Casual Memecoin Builder"

**Sample Size:** 102 tweets (Jul 2025 - Nov 2025)

**Baseline Content Mix:**
- **Other/General**: 48.0% - Dominant category, varied conversational content
- **Media Links**: 28.4% - Frequently shares links
- **Engagement**: 12.7% - Sometimes asks questions or seeks input
- **Hype/Metrics**: 7.8% each - Occasional excitement and metrics
- **Shipping**: 3.9% - Rarely talks about launches/builds
- **Defensive**: 1.0% - Almost never defensive

**Style Profile:**
- **Short tweets**: 135 chars avg, ~23 words
- **CAPS heavy**: 37.3% use uppercase words
- **Minimal emojis**: Only 2.0% of tweets
- **Question rate**: 12.7%

**Content Evolution:**
- **Early → Late shift**: More metrics (↑6.2pp), less general content (↓11.7pp)
- **Interpretation**: Started casual, became slightly more metrics-focused

**Engagement Patterns:**
- **Best performing**: Hype tweets (3,663 avg likes)
- **Worst performing**: Media links (2,233 avg likes)

**What's "normal" for a1lon9:**
- Casual, short tweets without much structure
- Heavy CAPS usage
- General commentary rather than product updates
- Not particularly defensive or reactive to criticism

**Red flags for unusual content:**
- Long defensive threads (rare for them)
- Detailed product announcements (they don't ship much)
- Formal tone without CAPS

## Engagement Correlation Findings

### Highest Engagement Content Types:

1. **Hype tweets** generally perform well across all founders
2. **Defensive/FUD-fighting** tweets get high engagement (chameleon_jeff: 3,073 avg)
3. **Engagement-seeking** tweets (questions) perform well (theunipcs: 683 avg)
4. **Metrics** tweets perform well for technical founders (weremeow: 1,033 avg)
5. **Media links** often underperform despite being common

### Engagement Strategy Differences:

- **cz_binance**: Rare hype tweets get massive engagement (11.8K avg) despite low frequency
- **theunipcs**: Consistent engagement across all categories
- **weremeow**: Metrics and defensive content outperform casual tweets
- **chameleon_jeff**: Engagement and defensive tweets perform best

## Time Evolution Patterns

### Common Trends:
1. **Media sharing increases** over time for most founders (weremeow +38pp, theunipcs +51pp)
2. **"Other" content decreases** as founders professionalize
3. **Shipping content** tends to decrease after initial launch phase
4. **Defensive content** appears as projects mature and face criticism

### Founder-Specific Evolution:
- **weremeow**: Dramatic shift from builder to curator
- **theunipcs**: From conversational to marketing machine
- **chameleon_jeff**: Increasingly technical and data-heavy
- **pasternak**: From intense building to more casual content

---

## Usage Recommendations

1. **Baseline comparison**: Compare new tweets against these profiles
2. **Deviation scoring**: Calculate % difference from baseline distributions
3. **Multi-factor anomalies**: Flag tweets that deviate in multiple dimensions (length + tone + category)
4. **Time-aware**: Consider evolution trends when comparing early vs late tweets
5. **Engagement prediction**: Use category performance to predict likely engagement
